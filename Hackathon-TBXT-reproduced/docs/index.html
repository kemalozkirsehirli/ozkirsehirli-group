Computational discovery for chordoma and TBXT/brachyury biology.

Özkırşehirli Group builds AI/CADD, molecular simulation, and reproducible research workflows for rare-cancer therapeutic discovery. Our current public project focuses on chordoma research through TBXT/brachyury computational hit identification.

What we do

We develop computational pipelines that connect molecular docking, QSAR, generative chemistry, protein-ligand modeling, sourceability checks, and reproducibility tooling. The goal is to turn rare-disease therapeutic hypotheses into auditable candidate-selection workflows.

AI/CADD pipelines

Docking, GNINA-style rescoring, RDKit descriptors, QSAR, generative molecule design, and multi-signal consensus ranking.

TBXT/brachyury focus

Our hackathon-derived repository studies computational hit identification around TBXT/brachyury-relevant chordoma biology.

Open research infrastructure

We preserve scripts, reports, dashboards, candidate summaries, and release notes so collaborators can audit and extend the workflow.

Why chordoma?

Chordoma is a rare cancer that occurs in the bones of the skull base and spine. TBXT/brachyury is deeply tied to chordoma biology: public clinical resources note that nearly all chordomas have high levels of brachyury, also known as TBXT.
