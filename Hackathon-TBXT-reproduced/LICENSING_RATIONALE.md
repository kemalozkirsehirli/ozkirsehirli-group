# Licensing Rationale

This repository now uses `TBXT Research Source License v1.0` in `LICENSE`.

## Why a custom source-available research license

The supplied archive appears to contain work from multiple contributors and was
described as shared intellectual property. A standard permissive license such as
MIT, BSD, or Apache-2.0 would broadly allow public use, modification, and
redistribution. That may be the right choice later, but it is aggressive while
attribution, ownership, downstream compound IP, and maintainer authority are
still sensitive.

The current license is intentionally narrower:

- It allows viewing and local non-commercial research reproduction.
- It preserves attribution and provenance.
- It blocks redistribution, commercial drug-discovery use, compound
  procurement/synthesis/testing, patent-claim use, and endorsement claims
  without written permission.
- It grants no patent, trademark, clinical, regulatory, or third-party data
  rights.

## When to change it

After written rights-holder and institutional review, the maintainers can switch
to a standard open-source license, a dual-license model, or a commercial license.
Until then, keep this custom license in place.
