## Methodology Note (read before submitting)

We approached TBXT/Brachyury hit identification as a *consensus* problem rather than a single-model bet: every compound in our 24-pick list survives multiple independent lines of computational evidence pointing to the same site-F "pillar" pocket on Brachyury (PDB 6F59 chain A, around the G177D hot-spot). We co-folded each candidate with TBXT using Boltz-2, docked each pose with GNINA's CNN scoring, refined and re-ranked with MMGBSA in implicit solvent (OpenMM + GBn2), and cross-checked with Rowan's Vina/Vinardo docking plus a 5 ns explicit-solvent MD pose-stability run. In parallel, a TBXT-specific QSAR (random forest + XGBoost trained on 650 measured Naarmann SPR Kd values) gave a data-driven affinity prior, and a 16-paralog T-box selectivity analysis screened out hits likely to bind off-target T-box family members. Every surviving compound was then put through muni.bio's `onepot` tool — we kept *only* compounds with a 100% catalog match (similarity 1.000), known price, and acceptable chemistry/supplier risk — and through Rowan ADMET (49 properties), RDKit ESOL solubility, the chordoma chemistry rule (MW ≤ 600, LogP ≤ 6, HBD ≤ 6, HBA ≤ 12), and a lead-like ideal filter (10–30 heavy atoms, < 5 rings, ≤ 2 fused). The final hard gate ruled out anything covalent, anything with PAINS or forbidden motifs, and anything with Tanimoto ≥ 0.85 to the organizer's reference databases. The result is 24 compounds where each pick is supported by structure-based docking, biophysics-trained QSAR, MD stability, ADMET, selectivity, and real-world purchasability — not just one score.
